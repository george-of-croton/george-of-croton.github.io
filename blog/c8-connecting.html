Empathy

What is it? And why is it useful.

Empathy is the cornerstone of any emotional skillset. Empathy allows us to relate to and understand how others feel even when our feelings may be different. When understanding empathy it is useful to know what an absence of empathy would mean. Someone with out empathy would be known as a psychopath. Serial killers, school shooters, suicide bombers etc would be all be likely to have very low empathy. If these people did have empathy, they would understand the pain their actions would cause, making acts of violence unappealing. So here we can see that empathy is the ability to understand another humans pain, when we ourselves are not experiencing that pain. Once we understand someone else's pain, we can be me a lot more effective at providing that person with the consolation or understanding required to help them feel better, and less alone in their suffering.

In contemporary society, you will often hear the critique that technology is eroding our ability to empathise with others. That the abstraction of interacting with another human through a computer interface, be it facebook, skype, text message, snapchat, limits the quality of interaction and causes to an extent dehumanisation. I think that this argument is sad, and incorrect. Technology has allowed us to be more connected with our friends and loved ones than ever before. For example is a text message from a loved one overseas saying " i miss u " hold no emotional value? Would the absence of communication be more valuable here? I don't think so. I think it is easy to blame the degradation of empathy on technology, but is more likely to be our changing lifestyles. Long commutes, long hours, splintered communities etc are all factors in the degradation of empathy in contemporary life.
