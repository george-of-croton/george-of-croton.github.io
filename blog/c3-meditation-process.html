Meditation Process

Meditation has always intruged but I have never fuly applied myself to it, nor understood exactly how it works. As laid back kind of guy, the idea of sitting motionless and zoning out always seemed right up my alley. But during the rare times that i have tried to attain a more mindful state I get distracted and give up.

Reading the chapter I learned that distraction was a part of meditation practice and that all meditators experience this. This is when I understood the work of meditation. The work of meditation is the resistance of distraction and having the willpower to return ourselves to low stimulation and focussed state of mindfulness.

I find sitting cross legged in a cool dark room to be a good way of mediating. I have trouble sitting in the proper positions but I'm sure that is just a matter of practice. I am learning how sitting at a computer is not conducive to flexibility.
